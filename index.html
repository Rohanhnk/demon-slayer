Demon Slayer S1

Summary

The series sets Tanjiro up as kindhearted, while also noting he possesses a sense of smell superior to that of a normal person, allowing him to perform near-supernatural feats, such as smelling the blood of demons. After selling charcoal in town, Tanjiro returns to find his entire family has been killed by a demon, save for Nezuko. Tanjiro, who is also a remarkably fast runner, bolt toward the nearest town. However, Nezuko attacks him. As Tanjiro tries to fend her off, they're confronted by Giyu Tomioka, one of the show's titular demon slayers. He reveals Nezuko has turned into a demon, and attempts to behead her with his Nichirin Blade, the only weapon capable of taking down a demon, provided it decapitates the monster. Tanjiro tries to protect Nezuko, but Tomioka easily knocks him out. However, something bizarre happens: Nezuko, whose demon nature should make her desire to eat anyone, including family, steps in to protect her brother. Tomioka knocks out Nezuko rather than kill her.

After several days, Tanjiro's Nichirin Sword is delivered to him. Each of the swords takes on a different color depending on its user. Tanjiro's blade turns black, which is something of a cursed color, as those who wield that particular color don't tend to last long. Tanjiro and Nezuko (who is confined to a box during the day, as sunlight kills demons) set off on their first mission, which is relayed by the former's Kasugaigarasu, a crow companion who gives missions to the young demon hunter. Urokodaki explains to Tanjiro that a demon named Muzan Kibutsuji, who bears an uncanny resemblance to Michael Jackson, is the progenitor of all demons. That means he might know how to reverse Nezuko's condition. Tanjiro's quest eventually leads him to Asakusa, Tokyo, where he finds Kibutsuji, who is pretending to live as a human, going so far as to have a wife and child. However, the demon immediately proves himself capable and crafty, using his abilities to destroy anyone who gets in his way. After a confrontation with Kibutsuji gone awry, Tanjiro meets Tamayo and Yushiro. Although both are demons, they're actively working against Kibutsuji, as they're not subject to his control like others of their kind. Tamayo reveals she might be able to turn someone from a demon back into a human by using the blood of the Twelve Demon Moons, immensely powerful monsters who have been especially empowered by Kibutsuji.

Demon Slayer S2

Summary

The first part, titled Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba – Mugen Train Arc,[a] is a seven-episode recompilation of the "Mugen Train" arc as featured in the 2020 anime film. It contains new music and an all new anime original episode which takes place immediately before the main story. The first part aired from October 10 to November 28, 2021.[1][2] The second part, titled Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba – Entertainment District Arc,[b] aired eleven episodes from December 5, 2021, to February 13, 2022.[3] The season was directed by Haruo Sotozaki, with character designs by Akira Matsushima, and animation produced by Ufotable; staff and cast from the first season and Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba – The Movie: Mugen Train also returned.[4][5] "Mugen Train" follows Tanjiro and his allies as they join Flame Hashira Kyojuro Rengoku on a mission inside a passenger train to face a hidden demon and rescue the passengers. "Entertainment District" takes place after the previous mission, where they assist Sound Hashira Tengen Uzui to investigate the disappearances of Tengen's wives while gathering information about a demon who terrorizes the red-light districts of Yoshiwara. For the Mugen Train Arc, the opening theme is "Akeboshi" (明け星, lit. 'Morning Star') while the ending theme is "Shirogane" (白銀, lit. 'Silver'), both performed by Lisa. As is originally the case in the Mugen Train film, "Homura" (炎, Flame) is used as the ending theme for episode 7. For the Entertainment District Arc, the opening theme is "Zankyōsanka" (残響散歌, lit. 'Song of Reverberation') while the ending theme is "Asa ga Kuru" (朝が来る, lit. 'Daybreak'), both performed by Aimer.[2]

Summary

In the aftermath of the decimated Entertainment District, a battered Tengen Uzui emerges, having lost an arm and making the decision to retire immediately. Alongside him are Zenitsu and Inosuke, both comatose, and Tanjiro, who also succumbs to a coma shortly thereafter. Upon discovering them, A Kakushi swiftly signaled his colleagues and arranged for their transfer to the Butterfly Mansion, ensuring they received the necessary and proper medical treatment.

Demon Slayer S4

Summary

The Arc isn’t only concerned with Tanj’s training, but it will see the rest of the Hashira trying to awaken their Demon Slayer Marks in order to increase their strength and beat Muzan once and for all. During Demon Slayer season 3, we saw that Muichiro and Mitsuri gained their marks while fighting the Upper Rank 4 and Upper Rank 5 demons. With Tanjiro also having his Demon Slayer Mark ready to go, the rest of the Hashira need to join the crew if they have any hope of wiping out the demons once and for all. In order to awaken their marks, the Hashira must complete certain and (at times life-threatening) criteria – meaning they must push themselves to the limit to unlock their true potential.

What season will be The Hashira Training Arc?

The Hashira Training Arc will be covered throughout Demon Slayer season 4 and is the final stop before we begin the epic showdown between the Demon Corps and the demon lord Muzan. So yeah, basically, fans won’t want to miss this. While the Hashira Training Arc is focused more on the Demon Slayers internally than their fighting foes, it is a vital story beat to truly experience the upcoming finale. During this Arc, fans will get a closer look at all the Hashira’s backstories and will grow to love them more. Nothing is more exciting than watching anime characters you’ve just connected with be thrown into danger, So to fully appreciated the Demon Slayer finale, you’ll need to tune into Demon Slayer season 4.
